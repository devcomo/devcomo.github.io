March 20th, 2013 - Phono, WebRTC, and Rails Security

Mar 14th, 2013

The March meet up is on Wednesday, March 20th, hosted at Reynolds Journalism Institute. Show up around 5:30pm for networking/food. The talks start at 6. We meet in the glass room right in the main lobby.

Comms-enabling your apps with Phono and WebRTC

Brian Pitts

Phono is an open-source library that turns any web browser or mobile app into a phone or chat client. I’ll provide an overview of different communications protocols, including the emerging WebRTC standard, and show you how easy it is to use them via Phono.

Brian Pitts works remotely as a systems engineer for Voxeo Labs, where he follows the teachings of @DEVOPS_BORAT.”

Rails Security

Ian graves

Reviewing the Latest Rails Vulnerability, CVE-2013-0333 and Friends

The Rails framework was recently revealed to have a critical vulnerability that allowed for remote code execution on the server. Many commonly used iterations of Rails have been shown to have security issues because of these findings. This talk will step through what makes Rails vulnerable and how an exploit would work. It’s fair to expect a demo that includes a vulnerable Rails distribution, Metasploit, and cursing by Ian when it doesn’t work perfectly.

Bio:

Ian Graves is a PhD student in the Center for High Assurance Computing at the University of Missouri. His research focus centers around languages and security implications therein. Haskell is his jam, but Ruby’s okay too.
