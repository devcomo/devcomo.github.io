November 2012: Stripe Capture the Flag and Ruby on Rails

Nov 12th, 2012

November’s meetup is on Wednesday, November 14th! It will be hosted at RJI (directions). Come by around 5:30pm - the talks start at 6.


Note: This month’s meetup is at the Reynolds Journalism Institute, in the same room we had our workshop night a few months back.  The room is visible from the main lobby, with large glass windows.  Walk in the front door, and keep going straight until you can’t anymore.

Stripe Capture The Flag

By: Ian Graves - University of Missouri, Center for High Assurance Computing

About the talk: 

The Stripe Capture the Flag 2.0 contest was a web-based security contest put on by Stripe, the new and awesome payment processor.  The contest focused on important yet-frequently-overlooked security issues with web applications including SQL injections, XSS.

The CTF consisted of nine challenges for participants to work through. I plan on giving an overview of the contest as a whole and giving a live demo of a selection of challenges, time permitting.

About Ian

Ian Graves is a professional student PhD student at the University of Missouri studying security vulnerabilities and their relation to compilation techniques.  He spends most of his day writing Haskell and dealing with systems-level coding, but has been known to moonlight with Ruby and Rails.

Intro To Ruby On Rails

By: Joshua Smith - The Add Sheet!

About the talk: 

Ruby on Rails is an open source web framework for Ruby that allows for rapid development of web applications

About Joshua

Joshua Smith is the lead developer at The Add Sheet. He manages the full-stack for the addsheet.com Rails app. After hours, he dabbles in Backbone.js and iOS programming, usually including their integration with Rails.

 

Hope to see everyone at RJI this month!

October 2012: Massively Scaling to Millions of Players: A League of Legends Story

Oct 17th, 2012

October’s meetup is on Wednesday, October 24th! Here’s the gist: it will be hosted at our usual Museao location (directions). The talk will start around 6:00pm but come by around 5:30pm or so if you want to hang out beforehand.

Come join us on October 24th.  We are having an out of town guest from Riot Games come to present about scaling an online game to millions of users!

Massively Scaling to Millions of Players: A League of Legends Story

By: Scott Delap

In this talk, we will cover the challenges and lessons-learned of scaling and operating the PvP.net Platform that supports millions of League of Legends global players daily.

In addition to covering software architecture and design choices in building PvP.net, we will cover the often-overlooked (in the race to market) aspects of monitoring, deployment, analytics, testing and automation.

About Scott

Scott Delap is a scalability architect at Riot Games, Inc. creator of the award winning online MOBA game League of Legends. He focuses on architecture for large scale, fault tolerant application platforms. Scott is also the former lead Java editor for InfoQ.com. In past lives Scott is the author of the popular Desktop Java Live ebook and a seasoned UI developer. Scott is a frequent presenter at conferences such as JavaOne, QCon and No Fluff Just Stuff. He won a Java Rockstar Award for his 2006 JavaOne presentation on Swing Threading.

 

Hope to see everyone at Museao this month!

July 2012 - Capistrano and Sinatra

Jul 6th, 2012

July’s meetup is on Wednesday, July 18th! Here’s the deal: it will be hosted at our usual Museao location (directions). Talks start at 6:00pm but come by around 5:30pm or so if you want to hang out beforehand.

Come join us on July 18th as two of the ComoRichWeb organizers share their experience with some great Ruby tools and libraries!

Using Capistrano to Deploy Anything

By: James Carr

Still struggling FTP’ing content out to your servers? rsync have you down? Stop wasting your time and come hear about how you can use Capistrano to deploy anything to any server you have ssh access to with zero downtime, regardless whether your app is written in PHP, Rails, or Node.js. I’ve give an overview of Capistrano, the deployment lifecycle, and demonstrate deploying (and rolling back) changes on a live site. I’ll also briefly touch upon using Jenkins to deploy using capistrano. 

About James

James Carr is a local developer who works works on JVM based projects at Carfax by day and hacks on ruby and node.js projects at night. He’s an active open source contributor and passionate about enabling software development communities.

Sinatra: The Little Not-Framework That Could

By: Clint Shryock

Sinatra is a well known DSL for building websites, web services, and web applications in Ruby. At just around 2,000 lines of code, it’s the smallest, most versatile non-framework out there. In this session I’ll give you a basic intro on working with Sinatra (including a quick intro to Rack), show some useful features, and probably screw up some live coding.

About Clint

Clint Shryock is a Columbia native who currently telecommutes as a software engineer for Cramer Development in Iowa City, IA. He works in Ruby/Rails and PHP and has been writing web apps professionally since 2004. At night he gallivants between Ruby, Cocoa, and Riak hacking.

Hope to see everyone at Museao this month!

Startup Q & A

By: Brent Beshore and Brant Bukowsky

We’ll have a Q & A session with Brent Beshore and Brant Bukowsky. For those of you who don’t know, Brent and Brant are two major entrepreneurs from the local area and have started several successful area companies. Come on out and bring whatever questions you have on what it takes to get a successful startup off the ground.

About Brent

Brent owns AdVentures, which invests in communications-based companies. These investments range from ad agencies and media properties to a software development firm and a film company. There are currently 13 active investments and AdVentures was recently ranked #28 on the 2011 Inc. 500.

Brent is involved in the national startup scene, serving on the advisory board of Startup Missouri and recently joined Gen Y Capital Partners, a $30 million venture capital tech fund based out of NYC, as a Venture Partner.

Brent is the founder of the League of Innovators, which is a 16,000 sq. ft. co-working space and entrepreneurial incubator and involved in a number of non-profits as a board member including the Heart of Missouri United Way and the Community Foundation of Central Missouri. Brent’s efforts in launching the Joplin, MO Tornado Recovery Facebook page and raising $2 million for the relief efforts garnered him a 2011 VH1 Do Something Award nomination.

About Brant

Brant and his brother started their first company while pursuing education degrees at Mizzou and have gone on to start over a half dozen companies. Their first company, ShowMe Tickets, sold event tickets and was acquired in 2005 by a company that was later acquired by Ticketmaster. LakeRentals.com, an online vacation rental company, started that same year was acquired by The Weather Channel 18 months later. Their current company, Veterans United Home Loans, is a nationwide mortgage bank they started in 2002. It currently employs over 800 people.

The brothers have received the Inc 500 award, for being one of the fastest growing private companies in the US, for 3 separate companies. Brant was selected as a mentor for the Clinton Foundation’s Emerging Entrepreneur program where he mentored small businesses in Harlem, NY. He has been doing online marketing since 1999 and has spoken at national conferences regarding online marketing.

He is passionate supporter of entrepreneurship and lives in Columbia, MO with his wife and 2 boys.

Building Touch Apps with Web Technologies

By: Luke Daffron

About the talk:

If you are already familiar with HTML5, Javascript, and JQuery – you have most of the tools you need to write “native” apps for iOS and Android. If nothing else, it can help you build a really fast cross-platform prototype or V1. I’ll take you through how I developed an app with JQuery Mobile and PhoneGap Build, and share some tips along the way.

Task Queues in Python, Ruby and More!

By: Bryan Helmig

About the talk:

Task Queues are efficient ways to perform important things in the background instead of introducing delays for the user. Primarily, we’ll cover identifying use cases and architecture, but we’ll also dig down into Python’s Celery, Ruby’s Resque, and touch on some simple “home grown” queue systems.

 

About Our Speakers

Luke Daffron works locally for Foliotek, Inc and can be found as “LJD” on the AppStore. He moved to Columbia from St. Joseph to go to MU, and was hired by LANIT Consulting after college. He survived the company’s “consulting era” to help launch and support their namesake project. He’s a man-of-many-hats – splitting time between programming, usability work/testing, analytics/seo/split testing, project planning, and mentoring.

Bryan:

Bryan Helmig is a Missouri native and self taught hacker. By day he is the only Python/Django dev at Veterans United and by night he wrestles the backend that makes Zapier go. You can also find him on twitter as @bryanhelmig.

March Meetup: Hadoop+HBase and Backbone.js

Mar 7th, 2012

We have a great meetup planned this month for Wednesday, March 21st!  This will be hosted at our usual Museao location (directions).  Talks start at 6:00pm but come by around 5:30pm or so if you would like to socialize beforehand.

High Performance Messaging with RabbitMQ

James Carr will be presenting on rabbitMQ, messaging patterns, and why using databases for messaging generally sucks. 

Haddop and HBase for Managing Big Data

First up we have John Riley, Principal Consultant of Incite Retail who will be sharing with us his experiences with Haddop and HBase for Managing Big Data:

Big Data quite simply swamps Traditional database & Data Warehouse architectures. In the Big Data age now breaking upon us, the sheer volume, velocity, and variety of information content now operant in the application space required a new class of technologies and approaches.  Hadoop & Hbase have arrived, been put to long use and tested under the most stringent conditions by major social media & retail concerns.

John Riley brings decades of engineering and consulting experience in aerospace, manufacturing, healthcare, biotechnology, and finance from engagements in the US and Europe. Prior to Incite Retail, John worked at Elan Pharmaceuticals developing work flow and data processing pipelines on large datasets. Currently, John provides thought leadership in the consulting practice focusing on data transformation, modeling, and serialization of data into Hadoop, HBase, and Hive.

Backbone.js at Zapier

And then after all the heavy hitting Hadoop and Big Data talk Mike Knoop will give a bit of a lighter talk on Backbone.js at Zapier:

Backbone.js is a javascript micro-framework which really propels the idea of “web apps” instead of “web pages”. At Zapier, we are using Backbone and similar technologies to write a fast, modular,  and complex frontend system which our customers use to create and interact with Zaps. Our frontend is constantly challenged to convey the complex notion of linking business web applications together using APIs – and we are using Backbone to achieve that goal. Other talking points include Backbone SEO, Backbone performance, Backbone architecture guidelines and using Backbone with RequireJS.

Mike Knoop is a Mechanical Engineering graduate student at the University of Missouri. From Wildwood, Missouri, he moonlights as a co-founder of Zapier hacking on Backbone, Cofeescript, and Sass. In past lives he has been seen as a Facebook Forum Moderator, writer for InsideFacebook.com, and Z80 assembly programmer for the TI-83/84 calculator series.

 

Come join us on the 21st! We will have a good mix of topics and it is a great place to meet up with web developers, designers, and entrepreneurs in Columbia.
